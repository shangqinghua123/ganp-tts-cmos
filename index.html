Audio Samples from "MLFP-TTS: MULTI LOSS FUNCTIONS CONSIDERED ON PRETRAINED TTS MODEL WITH MORE NATURAL PROSODY AND DURATION"

Authors: Liang Tang, Qinghua Shang

Abstract: The end-to-end non-autoregressive text-to-speech (TTS) model has been able to synthesize high-quality speech, such as Fastspeech2. The encoder of conventional TTS can already extract hidden representations of phoneme sequences. However, due to finite labeled corpus data, the TTS model is still prone to synthesizing speech with unnatural prosody and inappropriate duration. In this paper, we use a large-scale corpus trained pre-training language model (PLM) as the encoder to obtain a more robust hidden representation with context information. To further enhance the quality of the synthesized speech, we consider combining spectral convergence (SC) loss based on the acoustic aspect with VGG loss and total variation (TV) loss based on the image aspect. Experiments show that our proposed method can synthesize speech with more natural prosody, more accurate duration and less noise in a Mandarin test set.
